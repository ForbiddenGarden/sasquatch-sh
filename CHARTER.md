# Snohomish Sasquatch Hackers (SSH)
### Founding Charter

---

## Who We Are

Snohomish Sasquatch Hackers (SSH) is an independent, community-run cybersecurity group serving the Northern Snohomish County corridor — Marysville, Arlington, Lake Stevens, Snohomish, Northern Everett, and surrounding areas. We exist to give local security practitioners, students, tinkerers, and curious minds a place to meet, collaborate, learn, and build — on their own terms.

---

## Mission

To build an open, welcoming, and autonomous local security community where members pursue projects they care about, share knowledge freely, and connect with like-minded people in their own backyard.

---

## Core Principles

**Open by default.**
All group projects are released as open source. We build in the open so the broader community can benefit from our work. Project licensing defaults to MIT unless the project contributors agree otherwise.

**Members own their work.**
Work you bring to SSH remains yours. No project, research, or tool developed by a member becomes property of the group simply by virtue of being presented or discussed here.

**No gatekeeping.**
Members are free to pursue the projects and research they want to pursue. The group does not have veto power over what an individual or team chooses to work on.

**Low friction.**
We keep overhead minimal. No dues, no bureaucracy, no mandatory participation. Show up when it's useful to you.

**Local first.**
We prioritize building genuine relationships within our geographic community. Remote participation is welcome, but our identity is rooted in Northern Snohomish County.

---

## What We Do

- Monthly meetups (hosted at Snohomish County Library community spaces)
- Member research presentations and project demos
- Collaborative open source security tooling and research
- CTF participation and skills workshops
- Networking and community building

---

## Membership

SSH has no formal membership requirements. Anyone interested in security, privacy, hacking, or adjacent technical fields is welcome to attend and participate. Continued participation is membership.

We ask only that members treat others with respect and conduct themselves in accordance with responsible disclosure norms when working on security research.

---

## Projects and Intellectual Property

Projects developed under the SSH banner are published to the SSH GitHub organization and released under open source licenses. Contributors retain copyright of their contributions. The group claims no ownership over member work.

Members who present personal research under the SSH name do so voluntarily and retain full rights to that research.

---

## Governance

SSH is founded and initially organized by its founding member. As the group grows, decisions affecting the whole group are made by rough consensus at meetups. There are no officers, elections, or formal votes unless the group decides otherwise.

The founding principles in this charter may be amended only by consensus of active members.
